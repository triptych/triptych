## London Games Festival 2026 Opens Today — JRPG-Inspired Title in Official Selection

The **London Games Festival 2026** (LGF) officially kicks off today, running through **Sunday, April 19**. Now in its eleventh year, the event is supported by the Mayor of London and delivered by Games London.

Of note for JRPG fans: the festival's **Official Selection** includes a yet-unnamed title described as a *"modern JRPG-inspired game with a unique battle system, rich story, fantastic visuals and music by Motoi Sakuraba"* (known for his work on the *Star Ocean*, *Valkyrie Profile*, and *Tales of* series). The game will be fully playable at **New Game Plus**, the festival's flagship public expo, which takes place **April 16–17** at Exhibition Centre White City — the expo's largest venue to date after doubling its exhibition space this year.

The **Games Finance Market** (April 14–15) opens tomorrow for studio-to-investor pitch meetings.

## Arknights: Endfield Version 1.2 "At the Wake of Spring" — Launches April 17

Hypergryph's factory-builder action RPG **Arknights: Endfield** is receiving its **Version 1.2 update**, titled *"At the Wake of Spring"*, this **Thursday, April 17** at 12:00 PM UTC+8.

### What's in the Update

**Story:** The update centers on a critical chapter in the Wuling storyline, bringing players their first direct confrontation with antagonist **Nefarith** after an urgent summons to the Marker Stone area — a facility central to Wuling's stability. This is described as the conclusion of the Wuling story arc.

**New Operator:** **Zhuang Fangyi** makes her playable debut, blending lightning-based Electric damage attacks with swordplay combat.

**Exploration:** Additional maps and exploration content open up across the Wuling region, with the Test Area unlocking later in the update cycle as players assist with post-disaster restoration.

**Industrial Gameplay:** New outpost, resources, and production options expand the game's factory-building systems. Quality-of-life improvements touch progression, combat, and industrial management.

## Final Fantasy XIV Patch 7.5 "Trail to the Heavens" — Full Preview Ahead of April 28 Launch

With just two weeks until release, Square Enix has published a full breakdown of **Final Fantasy XIV Patch 7.5: Trail to the Heavens**, arriving **April 28**.

## Persona 30th Anniversary: Kyoto Concert This Weekend, Official Site Hints at "Next Chapter"

The **30th Anniversary Persona Symphonic Concert** continues this **Saturday, April 18** with two performances at **Kyoto Concert Hall** (matinee at 14:00, evening at 18:30 JST). The Kyoto program features music from *Persona 5 Tactica*, *Persona 3 Reload*, *Persona 4/P4G*, *Persona 5 Strikers*, *Revelations: Persona*, and *Persona 5/P5 Royal*.

The concert tour opened on April 11 in Tokyo to strong reviews. Two further weeknight performances remain scheduled in Tokyo on April 30 and May 1.

Separately, the **official Persona 30th Anniversary website** has been quietly updated with language referencing *"the next chapter for the series"* — fueling ongoing speculation about a *Persona 6* or major new Persona project reveal. Producer Kazuhisa Wada previously confirmed that 2026 would bring "opportunities to talk about future developments for the series," and Sega Sammy Holdings' investor filings list **Persona 4 Revival** for launch within the current fiscal year (April 2026 – March 2027).

## Xenoblade Chronicles X: Definitive Edition — Switch 2 Physical Release April 16

As noted in yesterday's "Looking Ahead" section, the **physical edition** of *Xenoblade Chronicles X: Definitive Edition — Nintendo Switch 2 Edition* arrives at retail this **Thursday, April 16** via the My Nintendo Store and select retailers. The digital version has been available since February.

## The Weeping Swan: Ten Days of the City's Fall — Out Now on PC

Dark historical visual novel RPG **The Weeping Swan: Ten Days of the City's Fall** launched on **Steam on April 2** ($11.99 / £9.99 / €11.79) from developer ZerocreationGame and publisher 2P Games — the team behind the **million-selling VN The Hungry Lamb**.

Set during a brutal siege in 1645, the narrative weaves authentic historical eyewitness accounts with dark fantasy elements inspired by *Journey to the West*. The **450,000-word script** leads to multiple main endings and dozens of divergent outcomes. A demo was available during Steam Next Fest ahead of launch.

While the game leans more visual novel than traditional JRPG, its RPG-adjacent structure and roots in the Chinese indie JRPG-VN scene make it relevant for the genre's wider audience.
