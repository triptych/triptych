## Today's Launches

**Pokémon Champions** (The Pokémon Company — Switch, Switch 2 | free-to-start)
Launches today. Not a traditional JRPG — it's a free-to-play competitive battle simulator that lets players import Pokémon from Pokémon HOME and other mainline games to build teams for online play. Standard type/ability/move mechanics intact. Switch 2 players receive a free visual upgrade. A mobile (iOS/Android) release follows later in 2026.

---

## Coming Soon

**Traysia** (Telenet Japan / Ratalaika Games × Shinyuden — PS5, Xbox Series, PS4, Xbox One, Switch, PC | April 24, 2026)
The notoriously hard-to-emulate 1992 Sega Genesis RPG is getting a multiplatform re-release. Follows Roy, a boy dreaming of adventure, and Traysia, the girl waiting for him in their port hometown of Johanna. Five scenarios, full fantasy-romance structure. The re-release adds rewind and turbo speed functions. A curiosity for classic JRPG historians — Time Extension notes it was one of the Genesis's worst-reviewed RPGs at original launch.

## Fan & Community

**Persona 3 Reload — "P3P Kotone Cutscenes" Mod: First Scene Released** (April 2)
A fan team has released the first completed cutscene for their long-running effort to add *Persona 3 Portable*'s female protagonist Kotone to *Persona 3 Reload*. The 65-second clip recreates Kotone's arrival at Gekkoukan High alongside Yukari, rebuilt from scratch since cutscenes in *Reload* are fully animated (a model swap is insufficient). The team reports it took two and a half weeks to produce. The mod works alongside the existing *P3R FeMC Reloaded* mod. More scenes are in progress.

## Academic & Industry Recognition

**Shujinkou (Rice Games) — GDC 2026 Concert Feature + Waseda University Lecture**
Rice Games founder Julian Rice delivered a lecture at Waseda University on April 7 in collaboration with Waseda Game Lab, titled *"Shujinkou, JRPGs, and Innovative Language Acquisition Elements in Video Games."* Separately, music from Shujinkou was featured in the GDC 2026 Festival of Gaming Concert Medley in San Francisco, performed before several thousand developers — alongside themes from *Hollow Knight: Silksong*, *Ghost of Yōtei*, *Indiana Jones*, *Peak*, and *DOOM*.

## Online RPG Updates

**Final Fantasy XIV — Patch 7.5 "Trail to the Heavens" Arrives April 28**
The final major patch of the Dawntrail expansion cycle lands April 28, in two parts (Part 2 follows in early September). Highlights: the long-anticipated **Beastmaster** limited job (melee DPS, one-handed axe, 50 tameable beasts), the conclusion of the *Echoes of Vana'diel* alliance raid storyline, a new Ultimate, the dungeon The Clyteum, and a new trial against Enuo (Normal + Extreme). Housing limits, storage expansion, and miscellaneous QoL improvements also included. A Letter from the Producer LIVE covering patch details is scheduled for April 17.

**Final Fantasy XI — April 2026 Version Update (Live)**
The April FFXI update went live this week, leveling up the Besieged system and revising the early-game player experience including an overhauled tutorial quest. Coverage at Nova Crystallis and Massively Overpowered.
