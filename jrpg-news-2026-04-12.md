## Level-5 Vision 2026 Craftsmanship Showcase: Everything Announced

Level-5 held its **LEVEL-5 Vision 2026 Craftsmanship** online event on April 10, delivering updates across nearly all of its in-development titles. Here are the JRPG-relevant highlights:

### Professor Layton and the New World of Steam — PS5 & PC Confirmed, Late 2026

The biggest reveal from the showcase: the long-awaited eighth main *Professor Layton* title is now confirmed for **PlayStation 5 and PC (Steam)** in addition to its previously announced Nintendo Switch and Switch 2 versions. The game targets a **late 2026 global release**.

Level-5 CEO Akihiro Hino shared a development update: *"We're nearly done with the core gameplay, and are currently focusing on the visuals and overall presentation. Voice recording for the Japanese version has wrapped, and work on localization for international releases is now underway."*

The game is set in **Steam Bison**, a booming American frontier town powered by revolutionary steam technology — one year after the events of *Professor Layton and the Unwound Future*. Notably, the theme song is being produced by **Joe Hisaishi** (composer of *My Neighbor Totoro*, *Spirited Away*), with lyrics and vocals by **Lilas (ikura from YOASOBI)**.

### Inazuma Eleven RE — Switch 2 Version Added, PS4 Canceled

Level-5 confirmed that the **Inazuma Eleven RE** remake (of the original 2008 DS game) is coming to **Nintendo Switch 2** alongside its existing PS5, Switch, and Steam versions. The PS4 version has been dropped.

The game — a full remake featuring the beloved football RPG formula — will launch with support for Japanese, English, Chinese, French, Italian, German, Spanish, and Brazilian Portuguese. No specific release date was given.

Additionally, **Inazuma Eleven: Victory Road** (the series' live-service entry) is receiving a **Switch 2 physical release in Japan on June 11**, bundling all content through the *Rising Bond* DLC.

### DECAPOLICE — Switch 2 Only, Switch 1 and PS4 Canceled

Level-5's long-delayed detective action RPG **DECAPOLICE** (originally announced for 2023) is dropping its Nintendo Switch and PS4 versions. The game will now release on **Nintendo Switch 2, PlayStation 5, and PC (Steam)**. No release window was given; it remains listed as "In Development."

DECAPOLICE follows a detective in a futuristic city, blending real-time action combat with investigation mechanics. It has now been delayed three consecutive years.

## Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave Receives Full PEGI 12+ Rating

*Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave* — the upcoming Nintendo Switch 2 exclusive set in the *Fódlan* universe — has received its **official PEGI 12+ content rating** in Europe, upgraded from its earlier provisional classification. The rating carries descriptors for **Violence**, **Bad Language**, and **In-Game Purchases** (suggesting DLC is planned).

A full rating from PEGI typically indicates a game is nearing a release date announcement phase. No specific date has been announced, but the timing signals Nintendo could reveal a window soon.

Separately, **Splatoon Raiders** also received its PEGI rating at the same time, suggesting Nintendo may be preparing a wave of first-party Switch 2 launch window announcements.

## Aretha Collection 1993–1995 Announced for PS4/PS5/Switch — July 30 Japan

Yanoman confirmed **Aretha Collection 1993–1995**, bringing three obscure Super Famicom turn-based JRPGs to modern consoles for the first time. The collection includes:

| Title | Year |
|---|---|
| *Aretha the Super Famicom* | 1993 |
| *Aretha II: Ariel no Fushigi-na Tabi* | 1994 |
| *Rejoice: Aretha Oukoku no Kanata* | 1995 |

The collection launches **July 30, 2026** in Japan on **PlayStation 5, PlayStation 4, and Nintendo Switch**, priced at **¥8,580**. A physical Deluxe Edition (PS5 and Switch only) is available for **¥16,280** and includes an illustrated booklet, large acrylic stand, jigsaw puzzle, and soundtrack CD.

No western localization has been announced. The *Aretha* series has never been officially released outside Japan and is notable for being one of the earliest JRPG franchises developed exclusively for the Super Famicom. The announcement was first leaked via early copies of *Famitsu* before Yanoman's official April 8 Twitter/X confirmation.
