# JRPG News — April 22, 2026

## Post-Launch Reviews: Dragon Quest Smash/Grow

Reviews are now rolling in for *Dragon Quest Smash/Grow*, which launched yesterday on iOS and Android. The reception is mixed-to-middling:

- **Pocket Gamer** summed it up as "Not quite a true hero, but not a villain either" — noting it adapts the series' charm into a bite-sized roguelite loop that respects player time, but doesn't reach the heights of mainline entries.
- **Pocket Tactics** called the gameplay loop "intoxicating in that Skinner-box kinda way" with strong "one more run" appeal.
- **Ingamenews** was harsher, criticizing the gacha mechanics as "the digital equivalent of a slot machine draped in Dragon Quest nostalgia."
- The game currently holds a **3.0 on Google Play**, with some players reporting server authentication issues due to launch-day overload.

The consensus: accessible and charming for series fans, contentious for anyone wary of gacha systems.

## Upcoming Release: Ys Memoire: Revelations in Celceta — April 28 on Switch

Nihon Falcom and Marvelous have confirmed the full details for the Western Nintendo Switch launch of *Ys Memoire: Revelations in Celceta*, a remaster of the 2013 PS Vita action RPG *Ys: Memories of Celceta*. It arrives **April 28** (six days away) in North America and Europe.

- **Digital price:** $29.99 on the Nintendo eShop, with a 15% launch discount through May 4
- **Day One Edition (physical, $59.99):** includes the game, a 3D acrylic keychain/diorama set, a 2-disc OST with 36 tracks, five art cards, and a 21"×14" cloth map of Celceta
- The Switch version was previously released in Japan in May 2025

## Announcement: Manafinder II

Developer Wolfsden LLC has announced *Manafinder II*, a sequel to their well-received classic-JRPG-inspired *Manafinder* (currently available on PC, PS4, PS5, Xbox, and Switch). The sequel is set in a distant future relative to the original game and is targeting an **early 2027** release. No further details have been shared yet.

---

## Upcoming: Alabaster Dawn Early Access — May 7

Action RPG *Alabaster Dawn*, developed by Radical Fish Games (the team behind *CrossCode*), has confirmed a **May 7** Early Access launch on Steam. The Early Access build covers approximately the first half of the game's second story chapter, with around **10 hours of content**. The developers expect the full release — featuring seven main story chapters and roughly 40 hours of playtime — to follow after at least two years in Early Access.

## Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave Still on Track for Switch 2

Nintendo has reconfirmed that *Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave*, the 18th mainline entry in the series and a Nintendo Switch 2 exclusive, remains on track for a **2026 release**. No specific release window beyond the year has been given, and analysts suggest a summer or fall launch is most likely given the vague timeline. The game is set around a gladiatorial event called the Heroic Games hosted by a figure called the Divine Sovereign, and appears to be set in the same world as the Fódlan trilogy (Three Houses/Three Hopes), though in a different era — a figure resembling an older Sothis appeared in the reveal trailer.
