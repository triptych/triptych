# JRPG News — April 21, 2026

## Launches Today

**Dragon Quest Smash/Grow Goes Live**
The newest Dragon Quest title officially launched today on iOS and Android worldwide. Developed by KLab in partnership with Square Enix and supervised by series creator Yuji Horii, *Smash/Grow* is a free-to-play roguelite RPG built around randomized Blessing runs, classic Dragon Quest vocations, and four-player co-op. Combat is fast and mobile-friendly, and the main story centers on mysterious rifts appearing across the land. Yuji Horii described it in a new RPG Site interview as "a Dragon Quest experience that is highly intuitive and very easy to play on mobile," positioning it as a gateway to the mainline series. Pre-registration players received 10 Premium Transmuter Vouchers on launch.

Early reviews from MonsterVine call it "a decent mobile RPG for series fans" — solid but not a revelation.

**Wild Arms 4 Now Available on PS4/PS5**
The 2005 PS2 classic from Media.Vision is now live on PlayStation as of today, landing in the PS Plus Premium Game Catalog alongside *Horizon Zero Dawn Remastered*. PS Plus Premium subscribers can play immediately with modern features including save states and rewind. Push Square noted that Wild Arms 4's original PS2 reviews were "ruthless," so this re-release gives a divisive entry a second chance with a new audience.

**Cosmos Point Launches**
An indie JRPG with an emphasis on tough tactical decisions and battles released today. A demo has been available on Steam ahead of the full launch.

**Divineblaze: March Tactics**
A turn-based strategy RPG arrived on Steam today, blending board game mechanics with traditional JRPG structure.

## Interview Spotlight

**Yuji Horii on Dragon Quest Smash/Grow** (RPG Site)
In a new interview, the Dragon Quest series creator discussed the game's design philosophy: keeping the mobile experience intuitive while honoring the franchise's identity. He expressed hope that new players drawn in by *Smash/Grow* will explore the classic mainline entries.
