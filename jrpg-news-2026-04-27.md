# JRPG News — April 27, 2026

## Ys Memoire: Revelations in Celceta — Early Reviews Are In Ahead of Tomorrow's Launch

*Ys Memoire: Revelations in Celceta* touches down on Nintendo Switch tomorrow (April 28), and review embargoes have lifted. The consensus so far: an enjoyable but dated action-RPG that newcomers will appreciate more than series veterans hoping for sweeping modernization.

**Nintendo Life** scored it **7.5/10**, calling it "A Simple Yet Fun Adventure." The review praised the explosive combat, charming cast, and booming rearranged soundtrack while flagging a forgettable plot and a structure that shows its Vita-era age. **CGMagazine** echoed the sentiment, noting the combat and characters compensate for a thin story.

On the technical side, the remaster runs at 60 FPS with HD textures and a fully re-recorded OST — a meaningful upgrade from the 2012 original. The digital version ($29.99) carries a 15% launch discount through May 4; the physical Day One Edition ($59.99) includes a two-disc soundtrack, art cards, and a cloth map.

## The Blood of Dawnwalker Sets April 28 Showcase — Release Date Incoming

Rebel Wolves and Bandai Namco will host a roughly **45-minute "Road to Launch" stream tomorrow, April 28, at 9 AM PDT / 6 PM CEST**, where they will finally reveal the release date for *The Blood of Dawnwalker*.

The showcase will include:
- **New open-world gameplay footage**
- **A brand-new story trailer**
- **PC system requirements**
- Developer commentary on the game's narrative design and open-world structure
- Music from the soundtrack

*The Blood of Dawnwalker* is a single-player dark fantasy action-RPG set in a medieval world steeped in vampire lore. Developed by Rebel Wolves — a studio founded by veterans of CD Projekt Red — it targets PC (Steam), PS5, and Xbox Series X|S. While not a traditional JRPG, its strong story focus and Bandai Namco publishing deal have kept it firmly on JRPG-adjacent radar since its reveal. The stream airs on YouTube and Twitch.

## Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave Receives Official PEGI 12 Rating — Summer Window Implied

*Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave* — the eighteenth mainline entry in Nintendo's long-running strategy-RPG series and a Nintendo Switch 2 exclusive — has quietly received an official **PEGI 12 rating** from Nintendo UK, with content descriptors for violence, bad language, and in-game purchases.

PEGI ratings are typically filed within weeks or months of a game's release, and Nintendo's own financial documentation continues to list the title for a **2026 launch**. Leaks corroborated by reliable sources point to a **summer 2026** window, which the PEGI filing now lends credibility to.

The game centers on a gladiatorial tournament called the **Heroic Games**, presided over by a figure known as the **Divine Sovereign**. The setting appears connected to Fódlan (the world of *Three Houses* and *Engage*) but takes place in a different era — a figure resembling an aged Sothis appeared briefly in the September 2025 reveal trailer, fueling speculation among the series' lore community.

No specific release date has been confirmed by Nintendo.

## Granser – Act 1 Keeps Earning Praise Three Weeks After Launch

*Granser – Act 1*, the indie JRPG from solo developer Matteo Sansoni, launched on Steam on **March 25** and has continued to accumulate coverage and word-of-mouth through April. A recent GamesRadar write-up — headlined *"This mechs-and-airships JRPG is finally out after almost 4 years, and it's getting great reviews"* — brought the game to a wider audience.

The pitch: a **living overworld** with real-time weather, air traffic, and dynamic trade, layered over a turn-based combat system with active timing mechanics. Act 1 offers an estimated **15–20 hours** of story content alongside a suite of side activities including airship battles, sky fishing, hover racing, cooking, and monster hunting. The full release is planned across multiple acts.

Turn Based Lovers has featured Granser repeatedly in its weekly roundups, and the Steam user reviews remain Mostly Positive. For players who bounced off the backlog of big-budget JRPGs this spring, it's worth a look at its modest price point.
