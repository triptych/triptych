## April Fools' Aftermath

**NieR: Cosmic Horror** (Square Enix / Yoko Taro)
The official NieR Japan X account posted a cryptic teaser at midnight on April 1 titled "NieR New Project Production Confirmed" — except the kanji for "confirmed" (決) had been swapped with 血 ("blood"), a horror-inflected pun. The 20-second clip showed Emil's grin dissolving into a Xenomorph-like biomechanical figure, sending the fandom into a frenzy. Square Enix confirmed later in the day it was an April Fools' gag. Yoko Taro, who spent the evening posting about pho noodles, added a follow-up tweet noting it's "hard to tell what's real" — leaving a small ember burning for fans who suspect an actual NieR project is quietly in development. Square Enix had stated a couple of months ago the series would continue in some form.

## Recently Released

**Code Vein II** (Bandai Namco — released January 30, 2026 | PC, PS5, Xbox Series X|S)
The sequel to the 2019 anime-soulslike introduces the Luna Rapacis, a threat that turns Revenants monstrous. A time-travel mechanic sends players to the past to avert catastrophe, with blood-code customization ensuring no two builds feel alike. The expanded companion system allows real-time coordinated attacks. Multiple endings encourage replayability.

**Dragon Quest VII Reimagined** (Square Enix — released February 5, 2026 | Nintendo Switch 2, PS5, PC)
A full remake of the 2000 PlayStation classic with diorama-style visuals and Toriyama-inspired character art. Notable changes include pacing improvements to the notoriously slow early hours, a **shared inventory** (a first for the franchise), difficulty options, and a new **Moonlight mechanic** that lets characters equip two Vocations simultaneously.

**Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection** (Capcom — released March 13, 2026 | PS5, Xbox Series X|S, Switch 2, PC)
The latest entry in Capcom's turn-based monster-taming spin-off. A Rathalos egg hatches twin dragons claimed by rival kingdoms Azuria and Vermeil, drawing the protagonist into a war. Open-world exploration, Monstie bonding, and the series' rock-paper-scissors battle system return. Notable for launching on Switch 2 alongside the other platforms at launch.

## April 2026 Releases

**Granser – Act 1** (April 2026)
A JRPG with a living overworld: dynamic weather, trade economies, and real-time airship battles exist alongside traditional turn-based dungeon combat. Despite the "Act 1" naming, the game ships with thirteen chapters plus side quests. Strong early word of mouth on its story and world reactivity.

**Fight Life: Vanguard** (April 10, 2026)
A tactical JRPG emphasizing handcrafted, tight-quarters encounters where positioning and every move matter.

**EvoCreo 2**
A spiritual successor to classic creature-collecting RPGs, featuring 300+ monsters (nearly twice the original roster), traits, equipment, and an expanded move list. Directly aims at the nostalgic Pokémon audience.

**Spark Ignites** (Early Access)
An indie JRPG entering Early Access with its full main story already playable. New regions and systems will be added over time. Emphasizes player choice and a robust battle system.

## New Details on Upcoming Titles

**Final Fantasy VII Remake Part 3** (Square Enix — release TBD)
Developers have been publicly vocal about progress. Speculation points to a reveal at **Summer Games Fest 2026**, though the release itself may slip to 2027 to align with the series' 30th anniversary.

**Octopath Traveler 0** (Square Enix — TBA)
A new entry in the HD-2D series serving as a prequel, reusing assets from mobile title *Octopath Traveler: Champions of the Continent*. Features several CotC characters alongside new ones and a custom-created protagonist. Break and Boost combat and Path Actions return alongside a new **Town Building** mechanic.

**SacriFire** (Obsidian Entertainment / RPG Dev — TBA)
Described as a fusion of *Vagrant Story*, *Xenogears*, and other 90s JRPGs. Uses a 2.5D visual presentation and a combat system where players target individual enemy body parts and string combo attacks for bonus damage. Soundtrack composed by **Motoi Sakuraba** (*Star Ocean*, *Tales of*, *Dark Souls*).

**Trails Beyond the Horizon** (Nihon Falcom / NIS America — 2026)
Confirmed as the "beginning of the end" of the long-running Trails saga. A direct narrative sequel to both *Trails through Daybreak II* and the *Trails in the Sky* arc. Releases on PS5, PS4, Nintendo Switch, and PC.

**Beast of Reincarnation** (Game Freak — TBA)
Revealed at Xbox Games Showcase 2025, this marks a dramatic tonal departure for Game Freak. A post-apocalyptic action RPG following an outcast warrior through a vast open world; visually draws comparisons to *Horizon Zero Dawn* and *Princess Mononoke*. Features AAA production values and potentially Soulslike-influenced combat against giant beasts and robots.

## Pokémon

**Pokémon Winds and Pokémon Waves** (Game Freak / Nintendo / The Pokémon Company — 2027 | Nintendo Switch 2 exclusive)
Announced February 27, 2026 during the franchise's 30th anniversary Pokémon Presents. The first **Generation 10** entries, set in a tropical archipelago inspired by Southeast Asia with accessible underwater areas. The three starters are grass-type chick **Browt**, fire-type Pomeranian **Pombon**, and water gecko **Gecqua**. **Brazilian Portuguese** joins the series as a supported language for the first time. Early trailer footage has been called the best-looking mainline Pokémon has ever appeared.
