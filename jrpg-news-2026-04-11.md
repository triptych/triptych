## Square Enix Reports Updated Franchise Sales Milestones

Square Enix released updated cumulative sales figures for its flagship RPG franchises. As of early 2026:

- **Final Fantasy**: 209 million units sold (up from 204M as of June 2025)
- **Dragon Quest**: 97 million units sold (up from 95M as of June 2025)
- **Kingdom Hearts**: 38 million units sold

The Dragon Quest figure is particularly notable heading into the series' **40th anniversary in 2026**, a milestone Square Enix has signaled will come with celebrations and new announcements throughout the year. Dragon Quest VII: Reimagined (released February 5 on Switch 1 & 2) is the most recent mainline entry contributing to those numbers.

## Xenoblade Chronicles X: Definitive Edition — Switch 2 Physical Release April 16

Nintendo surprised fans last week by announcing a **Switch 2 Edition** of *Xenoblade Chronicles X: Definitive Edition* featuring 4K/60fps output in TV mode and 1080p/60fps in handheld. The digital version is already available. The **physical release ships April 16** — just five days away — and is available for pre-order now at major retailers for **$64.99**.

Players who already own the original Switch version of the Definitive Edition can upgrade to the Switch 2 Edition for **$4.99**.

## Fate/EXTRA Record: Delayed Indefinitely, Publisher Changed

TYPE-MOON's remake of the 2010 PSP cult classic *Fate/EXTRA* has been dealt another blow. After being pushed from its original window to Spring 2026, the game was delayed **indefinitely in mid-March**, with Bandai Namco Entertainment withdrawing as publisher. Development continues at TYPE-MOON studio bb under director Kazuya Niinou, but a new publisher and revised release date have not yet been announced.

The title remains in development for **PlayStation 5, PlayStation 4, Nintendo Switch, and PC via Steam**. This is a major setback for one of the most anticipated TYPE-MOON projects in years.

## Trails in the Sky 2nd Chapter: Fall 2026 Worldwide Confirmed

Nihon Falcom and GungHo Online Entertainment confirmed that **Trails in the Sky 2nd Chapter** — a full 3D remake of *Trails in the Sky SC* — will launch **simultaneously worldwide in Fall 2026** on PlayStation 5, Nintendo Switch, Nintendo Switch 2, and PC via Steam. The global day-one release follows the same model used for *Trails in the Sky 1st Chapter*.

The remake expands on the original with additional Quartz and skills, expanded side quests, new minigames (fishing, poker), and greater freedom to explore the kingdom of Liberl. A specific release date has not been announced; some sourcing points to September 2026 as a target.

---

## Digimon UP: Free-to-Play Mobile Monster-Raising RPG Announced

Bandai Namco revealed **Digimon UP**, a free-to-play monster-raising RPG for **iOS and Android**, during Digimon Con 2026. The game uses pixel art aesthetics and centers on the franchise's core Digivolution loop — players raise, feed, train, and battle with their Digimon partners from Baby all the way up to Mega Digivolutions. Digimon Cards and Digivices are also confirmed as features. A 2026 release is targeted, with no specific date yet.
