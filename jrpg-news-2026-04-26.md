# JRPG News — April 26, 2026

## Trails in the Sky 2nd Chapter Dated for September 17 — Japan Gets Exclusive Remaster Bundle

Nihon Falcom's *Trails in the Sky 2nd Chapter* — the 3D remake of the second entry in the beloved Kiseki arc — has been confirmed for a **September 17, 2026** worldwide simultaneous launch on PlayStation 5, Nintendo Switch 2, Nintendo Switch, and PC via Steam.

The game picks up literally one day after *Trails in the Sky 1st Chapter* ends, following Senior Bracer Estelle Bright as she searches for her adoptive brother Joshua and clashes with the secret society Ouroboros. Falcom is retaining the hybrid Quick Battle / Command Battle system from the first remake, adding new Crafts, a Brave Rush system, and a deeper Orbment build tree.

**The controversy:** Japanese physical and digital preorders include a download code for an HD remaster of the original *The Legend of Heroes: Trails in the Sky* — but this bonus is not available in the West. The likely reason: localization rights to the original Sky trilogy remain with XSeed Games, making it legally complicated for GungHo Online Entertainment America to bundle the remaster without separate negotiation. GungHo's official statement to Gematsu was terse: *"GungHo Online Entertainment America is not involved in these releases, and we have nothing to announce regarding the pre-order bonus in question at this time."* Western Trails fans are understandably frustrated.

## Kingdom's Return: Time-Eating Fruit and the Ancient Monster — Inti Creates' New Hybrid ARPG Is Out

Inti Creates (*Azure Striker Gunvolt*, *Dragon: Marked for Death*) launched **Kingdom's Return: Time-Eating Fruit and the Ancient Monster** on **April 23** for Nintendo Switch, Switch 2, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC via Steam at **$14.99**.

The game blends 2D side-scrolling dungeon-crawling with kingdom-building strategy: players dive into monster-filled stages to collect materials, then return to town to construct buildings, upgrade characters, and restore a fallen kingdom. The loop is designed so players can focus heavily on either side without locking themselves out of progression. A **Gal*Gun: Double Peace Character Skin Pack** DLC was also announced, releasing **May 14** for $4.99. Early reviews from outlets like Hey Poor Player and Technetbook have been positive, praising the tight core loop while noting the game is short.

## PRAGMATA Surpasses One Million Sales in Two Days

Capcom's sci-fi action-adventure *PRAGMATA* — following an ill-fated investigator and a young android navigating a lunar facility overrun by rogue AI — surpassed **one million units sold globally within two days** of its April 17 launch on PS5, Xbox Series, and PC. The Nintendo Switch 2 version launched **April 24** worldwide (Japan had already received it alongside the other platforms). Capcom called it a "highly innovative" gameplay fusion of action and puzzle mechanics. The RPG Site published a dedicated Switch 2 performance piece noting the port is solid but still needs a few patches to reach its full potential.

*Note: PRAGMATA is an action-adventure rather than a strict JRPG, but its sci-fi narrative and character-driven structure make it adjacent to the genre.*

## Alabaster Dawn (CrossCode Follow-Up) Enters Early Access May 7

Radical Fish Games — the studio behind cult favorite *CrossCode* — confirmed that their next game, *Alabaster Dawn*, enters **Steam Early Access on May 7**. The announcement was made during the Triple-i Initiative 2026 showcase earlier this month. The game is a fast-paced action RPG set on Tiran Sol, a world struck by the shadow of Nyx; players control Juno, an Outcast Chosen navigating a ruined world. Combat draws from *Devil May Cry*, *Kingdom Hearts*, and *CrossCode*. The Early Access build covers the first half of Chapter 2, offering roughly **ten hours of content**; Radical Fish estimates the Early Access period will last at least two years, with the full seven-chapter game targeting around **forty hours** of playtime.

## Ys Memoire: Revelations in Celceta Launches Monday (April 28)

A final heads-up: *Ys Memoire: Revelations in Celceta* — the remaster of Falcom's 2012 Vita original — lands on **Nintendo Switch on Monday, April 28**. The digital version is **$29.99** with a 15% launch discount through May 4; the physical Day One Edition ($59.99) includes a two-disc original soundtrack, art cards, and a cloth map. It's Falcom's second Switch remaster arriving ahead of *Trails 2nd Chapter* in September.
