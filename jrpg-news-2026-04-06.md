# JRPG News Summary — April 6, 2026

*New items only — see `jrpg-news-2026-04-02.md` for prior coverage.*

---

## New Releases & Ports

**Breath of Fire IV** (Capcom — Steam, April 1, 2026)
The classic JRPG launched on Steam at $9.99 (50% off through April 15). The release includes restored environmental audio, bug fixes, updated keyboard/mouse support, and minigame improvements. However, Capcom's retroactive addition of DRM to the package has drawn significant player backlash.

**Manafinder** (Wolfsden LLC / Ratalaika Games — Switch, PS4/5, Xbox, April 3, 2026)
The retro pixel-art JRPG (originally PC, 2022) arrives on consoles for $11.99. Set in the realm of Aevi, it features 23+ biomes, 120+ monsters, and runs 8–12 hours.

**HYKE: Northern Light(s)** (Blast Edge / Akatsuki Games & Aniplex — Apple Arcade, April 2–3, 2026)
The 2D pixel art action-RPG (originally released September 2025 on PS5/Switch/PC) is now on Apple Arcade for iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple TV, and Apple Vision Pro with cross-platform progress sync. Seven playable characters explore dangerous forbidden zones filled with magic.

## Release Date Announcements

**Another Eden Begins** (Wright Flyer Studios — September 17, 2026 | Switch 2, Switch, PC/Steam)
The enhanced standalone console/PC version of the time-traveling JRPG gets a firm date. A new trailer and 8 minutes of gameplay were released alongside the announcement. Features full English voiceovers, enhanced visuals, and a new cast of characters. Directed by Masato Kato (*Chrono Trigger*, *Xenogears*) with music by Yasunori Mitsuda (*Chrono Cross*).

**Traysia** (Ratalaika Games / Shinyuden — April 24, 2026 | PS5, PS4, Xbox, Switch, PC)
The obscure 1992 Sega Genesis RPG is being re-released on modern platforms. Additions include rewind/turbo, save states, screen filters, cheat codes, an art gallery, and a music jukebox. Available in Japanese, English, and Spanish. Curiosity factor is high — the original scored 13/40 in Famitsu.

**Xenoblade Chronicles X: Definitive Edition — Switch 2 Physical Edition** (Nintendo / Monolith Soft — April 16, 2026)
The physical Switch 2 Edition launches April 16 at $64.99 (digital has been available since February 19). Supports up to 4K/60fps docked, 1080p/60fps handheld. Switch 1 owners can upgrade digitally for $4.99. The cartridge contains the full game (not a key card).

## Live Service Updates

**Final Fantasy XI — April 2026 Version Update** (April 6, 2026)
The long-running MMORPG received its monthly update today: a revamped new-player tutorial covering Records of Eminence; a new way to obtain Skirmish rewards via NPC Oston; expanded Besieged content (level 119 support, level 13 beastmen army, new headgear rewards); and lowered map costs for new players.

**Final Fantasy XIV — Store Gifting Restrictions Lifted** (April 2–3, 2026)
Square Enix restored the ability to gift single-character items (mounts, minions, emotes, orchestrion rolls, fashion accessories, etc.) via the FFXIV Online Store — reversing a restriction imposed in October 2022 due to fraud/RMT concerns. Account-wide mounts remain excluded. Recipients must be on the sender's friends list for at least 3 real-world days.

**Dissidia Duellum Final Fantasy — "The Fight Rages On" Season Live** (April 2, 2026)
The new mobile team-battle game (launched March 24) held its first official livestream on April 2 and kicked off its new season running through April 28. New playable characters: **Onion Knight** (FFIII, voiced by Jun Fukuyama) and **Iroha** (FFXI, voiced by Inori Minase). Six new characters are slated across April–May.

**Final Fantasy IV Pixel Remaster — Xbox Game Pass** (April 7, 2026)
Joins Game Pass tomorrow on Cloud, Xbox Series X|S, and PC — continuing the monthly FF Pixel Remaster roll-out (FF1 January, FF2 February, FF3 March).

**Stranger Than Heaven** (Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio / Sega — Xbox Series X|S, Xbox PC, Cloud Gaming)
A new "Five Eras" trailer aired at the Xbox Partner Preview on March 26 and is generating ongoing coverage. The action-JRPG from RGG Studio spans five historical Japanese cities and eras: 1915, 1929, 1943, 1951, and 1965. Gameplay shows minigames, tram travel, romance systems, and food — set to a jazz-drumming soundtrack. A dedicated full broadcast is scheduled for May 6, 2026. ## New Announcements

**Rev.NOiR** (ILCA / Konami — PS5, TBA)
Announced at PlayStation State of Play in February 2026, *Rev.NOiR* is a brand-new JRPG from ILCA (developers of *Dragon Quest XI S: Definitive Edition* and *Pokémon Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl*) published by Konami. Features an amnesiac boy and a special girl working to stop "lightfall," a phenomenon that brings death. Produced by Nobuki Kadoi (ex-Square Enix: *Drakengard 2*). Receiving fresh coverage this week. No release date set.

## Anniversaries & Events

**Tales of Series 30th Anniversary — Ufotable × Production I.G. Illustration Project** (April 3, 2026)
Ufotable and Production I.G. announced a collaboration titled "ufotable×Production I.G ~RPG ga Tsumuida Kiseki." The two studios will create 188 new illustrations covering all mainline Tales titles, starting with *Tales of Phantasia* and *Tales of Xillia*, in both standard and SD/chibi styles. The first wave will be exhibited at Machi Asobi vol.30 in May. Ufotable also announced a live concert celebrating the 10th anniversary of the *Tales of Zestiria the X* anime, set for May 17, 2026.
