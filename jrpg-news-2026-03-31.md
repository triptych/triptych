# JRPG News Summary — March 31, 2026

*New items only — see `jrpg-news-march-2026.md` for prior coverage.*

## New Announcements

**Pokémon Winds and Waves** (announced March 2026)
The Pokémon Company officially announced the next mainline generation. The new region is divided into islands with a Southeast Asian aesthetic. A 2027 release window is being targeted, likely toward the end of the year.

**Granblue Fantasy: Relink — Endless Ragnarok** (July 9, 2026)
A major content update for *Granblue Fantasy: Relink* is launching July 9, bringing new characters, quests, and a brand-new endgame mode. The update ships alongside a Nintendo Switch 2 port of the game.

**Guns Undarkness** — *Shoji Meguro*
Developed by Shoji Meguro, legendary composer of *Shin Megami Tensei* and *Persona*. Set in a world on the brink of destruction, players control a private military company. Field traversal is real-time; combat shifts to turn-based once shots are fired.

## Recent Releases (Missed in Prior Coverage)

**The Crimson Tower** (March 2, PC — Steam & GOG)
A compact, party-based JRPG emphasizing fast travel, brisk encounters, and steady progression. Designed as a tightly scoped adventure for players who prefer shorter, focused experiences.

## Indie JRPGs on the Radar

**Saveseeker**
A meta twist on the classic JRPG narrative: you play as the daughter of the hero who saved the world, now on a quest to undo the damage he caused by reclaiming his corrupted save points — joined by former boss monsters from his original adventure.

**Runa**
Described as blending the best of *Persona* and *Final Fantasy*, with magicpunk visuals, social links, an engaging story, and turn-based battles. One to watch for fans of both franchises.

---

## Additional Details on Previously Covered Titles

**Lost Hellden** (additional details)
The game features music composed by Hitoshi Sakimoto (*Final Fantasy Tactics*, *Vagrant Story*), a Job System with 21 classes, and a card minigame alongside its eight-character roster.

**Ariana and the Elder Codex** (additional details — released March 26)
Side-scrolling action RPG where young librarian Ariana can physically enter books to repair corrupted stories. The Seven Hero Codices have been altered, causing magic to vanish from the world. She also uncovers the mystery of the Library and her parents' disappearance.

**Code Vein II** (additional details)
The plot involves time travel: your character travels to the past to find clues on preventing the world's destruction in the present. Multiple narrative branches and endings support replayability beyond the Soulslike loop.
