## Today's Launch

**Skautfold: Moonless Knight** (Red Art Games / Pugware — PS5, Xbox Series, PS4, Xbox One, Switch | $14.99)
The console version of the Lovecraftian action-RPG/Metroidvania hybrid launches today. You play as Gray, the 2nd Knight, dispatched on a diplomatic mission to the Dawn Empire of Japan that immediately unravels when Lunatic cultists instigate a religious coup. The series is reaching its conclusion — Moonless Knight follows *Shrouded in Sanity*, *Usurper*, and *Into the Fray*. Combat features nine swappable relics (grappling hook, bow, fire vampire summoning, teleport, etc.), a semi-open world with non-linear boss routing, and a stat-growth system tied to in-combat use rather than traditional XP. A Deluxe Edition is available via Red Art Games.

## Released This Week (Not Previously Covered)

**People of Note** (Iridium Studios / Annapurna Interactive — PC, PS5, Switch 2, Xbox Series X|S | $24.99 | April 7)
A full turn-based RPG musical from Iridium Studios, published by Annapurna Interactive. Players join Cadence on her journey to recruit a band of musicians and reach stardom. Combat works as a live performance: a "time signature" determines the number of moves per turn, and timing button presses to the beat amplifies attack power. The genre of the background music can shift mid-fight (rock, EDM, hip-hop, etc.), granting stat bonuses to party members whose musical style matches. The world of Note is divided into genre-themed regions including Rock City Durandis and EDM City Lumina. Voice cast includes Erika Ishii, Joe Zieja, Debra Wilson, and Jason Charles Miller. Reviewed positively at launch — Checkpoint Gaming calls it "a spectacular performance."

**Final Fantasy IV Pixel Remaster — Xbox Game Pass** (April 7)
The fourth installment in Square Enix's monthly Pixel Remaster Game Pass rollout arrived on April 7 for Premium, Ultimate, and PC Game Pass subscribers. The series has been running one title per month: Final Fantasy I (January), II (February), III (March), IV (April). The Pixel Remaster editions feature refreshed 2D graphics, remastered audio, and modernized UI while preserving the original game design. FF4's Active Time Battle system and its operatic story of Cecil the Dark Knight make this the most narrative-ambitious entry in the series to date.

## Release Date Set

**Another Eden Begins** (WFS / Studio Prisma — Switch 2, Switch, PC via Steam | September 17, 2026)
Release date announced this week. Originally a 2017 mobile title, *Another Eden Begins* is a fully standalone, premium reimagining — no prior game knowledge needed. Veteran scenario writer and director Masato Kato (Chrono Trigger, Xenogears) leads the project at Studio Prisma, with Yasunori Mitsuda composing alongside Procyon Studio's folk-orchestral arrangements.

Protagonist Aldo journeys across time and space to rescue his sister Feinne from an ancient evil, accompanied by 18 companions. The game features full English voice acting, 10 distinct endings, New Game+, and an enhanced visual presentation. Nintendo Switch owners can purchase an Upgrade Pass to transition to the Switch 2 Edition. A physical edition and serial codes for in-game bonuses were also confirmed.

## Spotlight: Konami's New JRPG

**Rev. NOiR** (Konami / ILCA — PS5 | TBA)
Worth a closer look now that more details have circulated since its February 2026 State of Play reveal. The PS5-exclusive action RPG is produced by Nobuki Kadoi — previously at Square Enix on *Drakengard 2*, *Gunslinger Stratos*, and *Lord of Vermillion* — and developed by ILCA, the studio behind *Dragon Quest XI S*, *One Piece Odyssey*, and *Pokémon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl*.

The world is afflicted by "lightfall," a natural phenomenon that kills those it touches. The story follows a boy with amnesia who meets a special girl, and together they set out to stop the phenomenon — the journey ultimately shaking the entire world. The title explores whether fate is decreed by gods or forged through human will. No release window has been announced.

## Western Release Confirmed

**Village in the Shade** (NIS America / Nippon Ichi Software — PS5, Switch, Switch 2, PC | Fall 2026)
Known in Japan as *Honogurashi no Niwa*, this Nippon Ichi Software title is headed west this fall, with a Japan launch set for July 30. Set in the mountain village of Kagatsu, the game blends life-sim elements (crop planting, homestead customization, befriending villagers) with RPG progression. A limited edition is available for pre-order via the NIS America Online Store at $89.99, including a collector's box, art book, DigiPak soundtrack, acrylic stand, five enamel pins, and sticker sheet.
