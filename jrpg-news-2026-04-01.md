## Releases

**Bravely Default: Flying Fairy HD Remaster** (Square Enix / Cattle Call)

## Delays

**Fate/EXTRA Record** (TYPE-MOON)
TYPE-MOON has delayed the long-awaited remake of *Fate/EXTRA* out of its previously confirmed Spring 2026 release window. No new release window has been announced.

## New Details on Upcoming Titles

**Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave** (Nintendo Switch 2 exclusive — 2026)
Nintendo confirmed the game is not at risk of slipping to 2027, but a specific release window still hasn't been pinned down — summer or fall 2026 seems most likely. New story details: the game centers on the **Heroic Games**, a gladiatorial contest hosted by the Divine Sovereign who promises to grant winners' wishes. Protagonist **Cai** participates only to save his dying father. The closing section of the announcement trailer featured what appears to be a grown-up **Sothis** from *Fire Emblem: Three Houses*, strongly implying the game is set in the same universe. *Fortune's Weave* is the 18th mainline entry.

**DECAPOLICE** (Level-5)
After multiple delays (originally announced for 2023, then 2024), Level-5's futuristic crime RPG is finally confirmed for 2026. Detectives investigate crimes in the real world, then enter **virtual crime simulations** to replay and solve them through turn-based combat. The studio's track record with Layton and Inazuma Eleven suggests strong narrative fundamentals.

**Deltarune: Chapter 5** (Toby Fox)
Toby Fox's Fall 2025 developer newsletter included a roadmap pointing to a **late 2026 release** for Chapter 5. Fox remains characteristically tight-lipped on content, but the roadmap confirms the chapter is progressing on schedule.

**Trails in the Sky 2nd Chapter** (Nihon Falcom / NIS America)
A full ground-up remake of *Trails in the Sky SC* (originally 2006) is in production, following the same approach as 2025's *Trails in the Sky 1st Chapter* remake. Estelle, now a Senior Bracer, pursues the organization behind the events of the first game, with the same real-time-to-turn-based hybrid combat and expanded party.

## New IPs & Indies to Watch

**Edge of Memories** (Midgar Studio)
Five years after their debut JRPG *Edge of Eternity* (2021), Midgar Studio returns with *Edge of Memories* — an action RPG set on a new continent in the same world. The Corrosion plague returns as the central threat.

**Étrange Overlord** (NIS America)
A new IP from NISA. Players control **Lady Étrange von Rosenburg**, a high-society woman falsely accused of assassination and condemned to hell. Rather than accept her fate, she sets out to dominate the underworld as a villainess. Features 4-person parties with up to 15 recruitable characters, and a **revolving lane** mechanic that lets players interact with environmental elements to debuff enemies.

**Parasite Mutant**
An indie spiritual successor to *Parasite Eve*, keeping the iconic action-RPG combat and bio-horror tone but transplanting them into a distant-future setting filled with psionics, mechs, and high tech alongside the expected mutants and monsters.

**Starbites**
A mecha JRPG about a team of scavengers surviving on a war-ravaged desert planet. Players battle monsters, raiders, and bosses between explorations of a ruined city. Bright, colorful art direction stands out against the grim setting.

**Arknights: Endfield**
The console/PC adaptation of the mobile Arknights universe brings real-time combat, base-building, and story-driven missions to a new platform audience. Expands the lore of Terra with narrative-focused content.

## Notable Adjacent RPGs

**Onimusha: Way of the Sword** (Capcom — 2026)
Capcom's revival of the Onimusha series. Set in a supernatural version of **Edo-era Kyoto**, players wield the Oni Gauntlet against demons and monsters. An action-RPG. Announced alongside a strong JRPG lineup for 2026.

**Zero Parades: For Dead Spies** (ZA/UM — PC May 21, PS5 TBA)
From the studio behind *Disco Elysium*, this narrative-driven RPG launches on PC **May 21** with a PS5 release to follow later in the year.
